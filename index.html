The is the flag of Bangladesh, often referred to as the "Red-Green" or "Bengali" flag, features a simple yet meaningful design. It consists of a deep green background with a large red circle positioned slightly toward the hoist side. The green background represents the lush green landscape of Bangladesh, while the red circle symbolizes the bloodshed and sacrifice made by those who fought for the nation's independence. This flag was officially adopted on January 17, 1972, after Bangladesh gained independence from Pakistan. It embodies the country's history, struggles, and aspirations for a peaceful and prosperous future.
